  • Georgetown Trailer Park Mall

    The Georgetown Trailer Park Mall is essentially a weekend market set in trailers with a focus on artistic, original, or hand-made goods.

  • Bettie Page House

    The Bettie Page mural is visible via Northbound I5 near the 65th Street exit.

    Learn more...

    743 NE 59th St, Seattle, WA 98105

  • A Sound Garden by Douglas Hollis

    Large scale musical sculpture located on National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration Western Service Center. Look for pipe like structures that whistle and howl when the wind blows.

    Note that you must enter through NOAA, provide a photo id, and have your bags searched. The Sound garden is also visible through the Magnuson Park Dog Park area, if you do not mind a partially obscured view.

    Learn more...

    7600 Sand Point Way NE Seattle, Washington, 98115 United States

    Enjoy an assortment of pre-colonial dishes including frybread, Indian Tacos (served on frybread), salads, and bowls.

Spotlight:

Hat n' Boots Park

Hat n' Boots was built in 1954 by businessman Buford Seals, as part of a Western-themed gas station named Premium Tex. The hat is 19 feet high and 44 feet across. The boots are 21.5ft tall and 24ft tall.

Oxbow Park, 6427 Carleton Ave S, Seattle, WA 98108
